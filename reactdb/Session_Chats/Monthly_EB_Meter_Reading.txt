## 🔄 What Happens Behind the Scenes

**Step 1: Meter Reading Saved**
```
POST /api/service-consumption
Body: {
  serviceAllocId: 1,
  readingTakenDate: "2026-03-31",
  startingMeterReading: "5000",
  endingMeterReading: "5100",
  unitRate: 15
}
```

**Step 2: Units Calculated**
- Total Units = Ending - Starting = 5100 - 5000 = **100 units**

**Step 3: Pro-Rata Distribution**
- Fetches all occupancy records for the room in March
- For each tenant:
  - Days Occupied = Check-in to Check-out (within month)
  - Pro-Rata Units = (100 units × Days / 31 days)
  - Charge = Pro-Rata Units × 15 rupees

**Step 4: Charges Stored**
- All tenant charges saved to `TenantServiceCharges` table
- Ready for viewing in "Tenant Electricity Charges" section

User: what will save and calculate charges do

GitHub Copilot: The **"💾 Save & Calculate Charges"** button does two things:

1. **Saves the Meter Reading** → Creates a new service consumption record in the database (`ServiceConsumptionDetails`) with:
   - The room/service allocation selected
   - Reading date
   - Starting meter reading (auto-filled from previous month or manual entry)
   - Ending meter reading (current month)
   - Unit rate (₹/unit)
   - Any meter photos

2. **Auto-Calculates Pro-Rata Charges** → Immediately triggers the pro-rata electricity distribution:
   - Calculates units consumed: `endingReading - startingReading`
   - Determines occupancy days for each tenant in that room during the billing month
   - Splits the total units and charges proportionally based on each tenant's occupancy period (check-in to check-out dates)
   - Creates tenant service charge records showing each tenant's pro-rata share
   - Stores: units owed, percentage of month occupied, charge amount, status

**Example Flow:**
- Room 101 consumed 100 units (ending 500 - starting 400)
- Tenant A checked in on 1st, Tenant B checked in on 15th
- Pro-rata splits: A gets ~67 units for 30 days, B gets ~33 units for 15 days
- At ₹15/unit: A charged ₹1005, B charged ₹495

Then you can view all charges in the **"Tenant Electricity Charges"** page to see the detailed billing breakdown.
